The Problem

An Invisible Emergency

Silicosis is a devastating occupational disease affecting millions in India's stone quarries.

The Scale of the Crisis

Estimates suggest that the number of Indian workers exposed to silica dust will touch 52 million by 2025. It is one of India's most urgent public health emergencies.

In Coimbatore alone, a study found patients had a mean age of 55 with an average exposure of 22 years—almost entirely devoid of risk awareness.

⚠️

Legal Urgency

In August 2024, the Supreme Court of India declared failure to prevent silicosis a violation of Article 21 (right to life). The government's approach has shifted from aspirational to obligatory.

The government now has a legal duty to act. DustGuard provides the required surveillance ecosystem.

The Solution

Four Dimensions of Innovation

⏱️

1. TWA Tracking

Competitors monitor instantaneous PM. DustGuard calculates a daily Time Weighted Average (TWA)—the recognized legal metric for occupational limits.

📶

2. No Smartphone Needed

Equipped with a SIM800L module, it sends SMS alerts entirely independent of smartphones, reaching users on even the most basic ₹500 phones.

❤️

3. Dual Bio-Sensor

Integrating a MAX30102 sensor monitors SpO2 levels. Declining SpO2 at the same exposure level proactively flags physiological lung strain.

🏥

4. PHC Integration

A cloud-powered ecosystem feeds longitudinal exposure data to PHC doctors, creating India's first field-level silicosis early warning network.
